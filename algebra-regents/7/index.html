Part 2 · Core Concepts

Anatomy of a Parabola 🦋

🎯 Vertex
The turning point — highest or lowest y-value. The parabola "bounces" here. Find x: x = −b/2a, then plug in for y.
📐 Axis of Symmetry
A vertical mirror line through the vertex. The left and right sides of the parabola are mirror images. Equation: x = −b/2a
📍 Intercepts
y-intercept: set x = 0 → y = c. x-intercepts (roots): set y = 0, then factor or use quadratic formula. A parabola can have 0, 1, or 2 x-intercepts.
A parabola is a perfect mirror — if you know one x-intercept is 3 units left of the axis, where must the other one be?

Part 3 · Graphing Steps

The 5-Step Graphing Blueprint 🗺️

①
Find a, b, c
Identify coefficients from y = ax² + bx + c. Check sign of a → opens up or down?
②
Axis of Symmetry
x = −b/2a  Draw a dashed vertical line
③
Vertex
Plug x from step ② into f(x) to get y. Plot (x, y) — the turning point!
④
y-intercept
Set x = 0 → y = c. Plot (0, c). Reflect across the axis of symmetry for a free bonus point!
⑤
Connect & Sketch
Draw a smooth U-shaped curve through all plotted points. Never pointy at the vertex!

Part 3 · Vertex Form

Vertex Form: The Power Form 💎

Vertex Form
y = a(x − h)² + k
Vertex is instantly visible: (h, k)
What each letter controls:
aWidth & direction: |a|>1 narrower, 0<|a|<1 wider; a<0 flips upside down
hShifts left/right: y=(x−3)² moves RIGHT 3 units (watch the sign!)
kShifts up/down: +k moves UP, −k moves DOWN
In y = −2(x+1)² + 5, what is the vertex? Which way does it open? Is it narrower or wider than y = x²?
⚠️ Sign Trap!
y = (x − 3)² has vertex x = +3. y = (x + 3)² has vertex x = −3. The sign inside the parentheses is the opposite of h!

Part 3 · Domain & Range

Domain & Range: The Boundaries 🔲

📏 Domain
All valid x-values. For every quadratic function, the parabola extends forever left and right — so the domain is ALWAYS:
All real numbers (−∞, ∞)
📏 Range
All valid y-values. The parabola stops at the vertex (min or max), then extends in one direction forever:
Opens up (a>0): y ≥ k
Opens down (a<0): y ≤ k
A ball thrown upward reaches a height of 15 m at its peak. What is the range of the height function? Can the ball ever be at 20 m?
For y = −(x+3)² + 7:
What is the vertex? Domain? Range?
Part 4 · Real World

🏈 The Touchdown Problem

🏟️ The Scenario
A football is kicked and its height (in feet) is modeled by:
h(t) = −16t² + 48t + 0
where t is time in seconds.
Before any math — when the ball just launched, what is its height? When it lands, what will h(t) equal? Can you estimate the peak time?
Solve step by step:
a) What is a? b? c? Opens up or down?
b) At what time does the ball reach max height?
   (Hint: t = −b/2a)
c) What is the maximum height?
d) When does the ball hit the ground?

Regents Focus

4 Things to Know Cold ❄️

❄️
Axis of Sym. Formula
x = −b/2a. Period. They will give you standard form and ask for it every test.
⚠️
Vertex Form Sign Flip
y=(x−h)²+k → vertex is (h, k) NOT (−h, k). Students lose 1 in 3 points here.
🔲
Domain vs Range
Domain is ALWAYS all reals. Range starts at vertex k and goes ≥k or ≤k based on direction of opening.
📈
a > 0 vs a < 0
Positive a → minimum at vertex. Negative a → maximum at vertex. Real-world problems will ask "what is the max height?" — that is always the vertex y-value when a < 0.
