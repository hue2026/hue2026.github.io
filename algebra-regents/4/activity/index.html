Part 3 — Substitution Method

4 pts

Isolate → Substitute → Solve → Back-substitute → Check BOTH equations. Show every step.

3A  Level 1
y = x + 4   and   y = 3x
Hint: Both equal y — set them equal!
Solution:
3B  Level 2
2x + y = 10   and   y = x + 1
Hint: y is already isolated in Eq. 2.
Solution:
3C  Level 3
3x + 2y = 16   and   x = y + 2
Hint: x is isolated in Eq. 2 — swap it into Eq. 1.
Solution:
⚠️ Substitution Trap — Don't Do This
After finding x, always substitute back into the original equation — not a rearranged version you wrote during your work. Rearranged expressions are where arithmetic errors hide.

⚠️ The #1 Regents Elimination Error
When multiplying an equation by a number, multiply every term — including the constant on the right side. If you scale "2x + 3y = 11" by 2, the 11 must become 22.

Part 6 — Regents-Style Multiple Choice

5 pts

One correct answer per question. Read every choice before selecting. Watch for Regents traps.

1. Which point is the solution to the system y = 2x − 3 and y = −x + 6?
2. A system of two linear equations has the same slope and the same y-intercept. How many solutions does it have?
3. Solving 3x + y = 7 and y = 2x − 3 by substitution, what is the correct first step?
4. To eliminate the y-variable from 2x + 5y = 14 and 6x − 5y = 10, what do you do?
5. Two lines are described by y = 4x − 1 and 8x − 2y = 2. Which best describes this system?
