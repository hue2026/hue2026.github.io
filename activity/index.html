💡 Part 4 — Want vs. Need
The Heart of Your Character
The best anime characters want one thing on the surface — but need something deeper. That gap is where your theme lives.
🎯 What does your character WANT?

The external goal — visible, concrete, and driving the story forward. What are they chasing?

Examples by genre:

⚡ Shōnen: become the best / protect their friends
🌸 Shōjo: be understood / fix a broken relationship
🌀 Isekai: gain power / find a way home
☀️ Slice of Life: keep the club alive / make a friend
🤖 Mecha: win the war / prove their worth

💜 What does your character secretly NEED?

The internal truth — emotional, tied to your theme, and often something they won't admit. What must they learn?

Connect this to your theme:

If your theme is belonging → they need to stop seeing themselves as an outsider
If your theme is perseverance → they need to believe their effort has worth
If your theme is reinvention → they need to forgive their old self

How does your character's FLAW get in the way of getting what they need?
🔍 Part 5 — Genre Fit
How Does Your Character Fit Their Genre?
Genre isn't just a category — it shapes how a character looks, acts, speaks, and what kind of story they belong in.
Visual Style Check

From the lesson clips — every genre has a distinct visual energy.

What visual style fits your genre? (bold, soft, dark, colorful, mechanical…) How does your character's appearance match that style?
Audience & Tone

Genre sets what the audience expects to feel.

What emotion should the audience feel when they first meet your character? What makes your character feel DIFFERENT from a typical character in this genre?
Imagine your character appears in a 30-second trailer. What do we see and hear first?
🌏 Part 6 — Culture Connection
Your Character & Japanese Values
The themes in anime aren't random — they come from real Japanese cultural values. Your character should carry one of these values, even if they struggle against it.
Cultural Values from Today's Lesson
Ganbaru (頑張る) Do your absolute best, always — perseverance as a daily value, not just a battle cry
Group Over Self Loyalty to the team, club, or family unit; individual success means less without your group
Respect for Nature & Spirits Shinto roots — balance, care for the natural world, and awareness of forces beyond the visible
Identity & Belonging The struggle to find your place — from Naruto as an outsider to Isekai heroes reborn in new worlds
Which cultural value does your character carry? How does it show up in who they are? Does your character embrace this value, resist it, or struggle with it? Explain. Could a character like yours exist in YOUR own culture? What would be the same — and what would change?
