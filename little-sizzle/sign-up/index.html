📋 Before You Begin

What do you think you need to sign up?

🤔 Before I tell you — what would you guess is required? Write down your answer, then compare below.

✅ You DO need:

  • A web browser (Chrome, Firefox, Safari…)
  • Internet connection
  • An email address (yours or a parent's)
  • A unique username — not your real name!
  • A password you can remember
  • Your birth month & year
  • Your country

❌ You do NOT need:

  • Any money — it's completely FREE
  • Coding experience
  • A special computer or app
  • Your real full name
  • A phone number

🔒 Privacy Tip:

Scratch keeps your birthday private — they use it only to follow child privacy laws (COPPA).

📌 Head to scratch.mit.edu in your browser to get started — no download needed!

🎭 Step 2 of 5

Choose Your Username & Password

🤔 Why NOT use your real name?

Scratch is a public community. Usernames are visible to everyone. What could go wrong if you used your full real name?

✅ Good Username Ideas:

CodeWizard42 StarCoder_99 PizzaRobot7 CoolCoder_j

❌ Avoid these:

  • Your real first + last name
  • Your school or address
  • Your phone number or birthday

🔐 Strong Password Tips:

✅

At least 6 characters long

✅

Mix letters, numbers & symbols

✅

Write it down somewhere safe!

❌

Never use "password" or "123456"

💭 Think about it: If the username "CoolCoder" is already taken, what would you add to make it unique? Hint: numbers, underscores, or a fun adjective!

💡 Scratch will check in real-time if a username is available — you'll see a green ✓ or red ✗.

🗓️ Step 3 of 5

Birthday, Gender & Country

🎂

Birth Month & Year

Select from dropdown menus. Day is not required.

Why do they ask?

Privacy laws (COPPA) require it. Your birthday stays private — no one else can see it.

🧑

Gender

You can choose Female, Male, Non-binary, Another gender, or "Prefer not to say."

🤔 Why does Scratch ask?

For research about who uses the platform. It's optional and private.

🌍

Country

Select from a dropdown list of 200+ countries. Used for regional settings only.

💡 Fun fact:

Scratch is used in 200+ countries and has been translated into 60+ languages!

✉️ Step 5 of 5

Verify Your Email — The Final Step!

📬 How to verify your email:

1

Open the email inbox you used during sign-up

2

Look for an email from no-reply@scratch.mit.edu

3

Open the email and click the blue "Confirm my email address" link

4

You're taken back to Scratch — account is fully verified! ✅

🔍 Can't find the email?

  • Check your Spam / Junk folder
  • Wait a few minutes and refresh
  • Make sure you typed the email correctly
  • Request a new confirmation email from your Scratch profile page

🤔 Before verifying, can you still use Scratch?

What features might be limited without a verified email? Think about sharing projects with others…

✅ What verification unlocks:

  • Share your projects publicly
  • Comment on other projects
  • Follow other Scratchers
  • Access your account if you forget password

🛡️ Stay Safe Online

Scratch Account Safety — What Rules Would You Set?

?

If you were designing Scratch, what safety rules would you create for users?

Think about: usernames, profile photos, comments, and sharing projects with strangers.

🎭

Username Safety

  • No real name or school name
  • No location hints
  • No contact info
🔐

Password Safety

  • Never share with anyone
  • Write it down privately
  • Use a strong mix of chars
⚠️

Community Rules

  • Be kind in comments
  • Never share personal info
  • Report inappropriate content

💡 Scratch has a dedicated moderation team that reviews flagged content. The Scratch Community Guidelines are at scratch.mit.edu/community_guidelines
