🖥️ Part 1 — Canva.com
Step-by-Step: Build Your Layered Illustration

canva.com
~25 min
🗂️
📌 Get started: Open your browser → go to canva.com → sign in → click "Create a design" → "Custom size" → enter 1080 × 1080 px → click "Create new design".
🌄 Steps 1–5 · Build the Background Layer

Start at the back — work your way forward

1
Add a background colour or gradient

Click Background colour (paint-bucket icon, bottom-left) → choose your sky or horizon colour. Or: click Elements → search "gradient rectangle" → drag one onto the canvas and resize to fill it.

2
Add background elements

Click Elements → search for clouds, mountains, stars, or distant buildings. Drag items onto the canvas. Make them smaller than you think — things far away appear small!

3
Open the Layers panel

Click any element → click Position in the top toolbar → select the Layers tab. You'll see your background items listed. Double-click a layer name to rename it (e.g., "BG Clouds").

4
Reduce opacity for atmospheric depth

Select a background element → click Transparency (chequered square, top-right toolbar) → set it to 60–75%. This "haze" makes it look further away. Foreground elements should stay at 100%.

5
Send background elements to back

Select each background element → Position → To Back. This ensures later layers always appear in front of your background.

🏘️ Steps 6–8 · Add the Middle Ground Layer
6
Add medium-sized elements

Click Elements → search for trees, houses, hills, or buildings. Place them in the vertical centre of the canvas. They should be larger than BG objects but smaller than foreground ones.

7
Apply the Rule of Thirds

Imagine a 3×3 grid over your canvas. Place your most interesting element near one of the 4 intersection points. Compare it to your thumbnail sketch — does the composition match your plan?

8
Arrange the middle ground layer

In the Layers panel, drag middle-ground elements above the background layers. They should visually overlap the horizon line slightly.

🌿 Steps 9–12 · Build the Foreground Layer
9
Add large foreground elements

Search Elements for your foreground items (person, large plant, rock, fence). Scale them up and position them near the bottom edge of the canvas.

10
Create contrast at your focal point

Select your most important element. Does it stand out from everything behind it? If not: change its colour, add a shadow (Effects → Shadow), or increase its size until it grabs the eye first.

11
Bring foreground to the front

Select each foreground element → Position → To Front. In the Layers panel, your foreground layers should appear at the very top of the list.

12
Check visual balance

Zoom out and squint at your design. Does it feel balanced left-to-right? If one side feels too "heavy", move an element or add a small object on the lighter side.

🖥️ Part 2 — Photopea.com
Step-by-Step: Advanced Layer Control

photopea.com
~25 min
🎭
📌 Get started: Open your browser → go to photopea.com → click "New Project" → set Width: 800 px, Height: 600 px, Resolution: 72 dpi → click "Create".
🗺️ Know the Interface First

Label the areas you'll use most:

Layers Panel (bottom-right)
👁️
Foreground
👁️
Middle Ground
👁️
Background

Higher in list = in front visually

Tools panel (left side) — Brush (B), Shape (U), Move (V), Fill (G), Text (T). Click any tool to activate.
Layers panel (bottom-right) — All your layers live here. Click a layer to select it. 👁️ hides/shows. 🔒 locks it.
Menu bar (top) — Use Layer → New Layer to add layers. Filter → Blur for depth effects.
Important: You can only draw on the currently selected layer. Always check which layer is highlighted before drawing!
🌄 Steps 1–4 · Create & Name 3 Layers
1
Name the Background layer

The default layer is your background. Double-click its name in the Layers panel → type "Background" → press Enter. Select the Fill tool (G) → choose a sky-blue colour → click the canvas to fill it.

2
Create a Middle Ground layer

Click the + icon (New Layer) at the bottom of the Layers panel. A new layer appears above Background. Double-click its name → rename it "Middle Ground". Make sure it is highlighted before drawing.

3
Create a Foreground layer

Click + icon again → rename it "Foreground". This layer must be at the top of the Layers panel. If it's not, drag it upward.

4
Test the eye icon

Click the 👁️ eye icon next to "Foreground" to hide it. Do you still see the other layers? Click the eye again to show it. Try hiding each layer one at a time.

🎨 Steps 5–10 · Draw Your Scene & Add Depth
5
Paint the Background

Click "Background" in the Layers panel. Select the Brush tool (B) → choose a large brush size → paint sky elements and distant shapes in the top half of the canvas.

6
Reduce Background layer opacity

With the Background layer selected, find the Opacity slider at the top of the Layers panel (shows "100%"). Drag it to 75–80%. This creates an atmospheric haze effect.

7
Paint the Middle Ground

Click "Middle Ground". Draw medium-sized shapes (hills, trees, buildings) in the centre section of the canvas. Try the Shape tool (U) for quick rectangles or ellipses.

8
Paint the Foreground

Click "Foreground". Paint large, dark elements near the bottom of the canvas. Use a colour with strong contrast against the background (e.g., dark shapes against a light sky).

9
Apply Gaussian Blur to the Background

Click the "Background" layer → Menu: Filter → Blur → Gaussian Blur → set Radius to 2–3 px → click OK. This softens the background, increasing depth. Do NOT blur the Foreground layer.

10
Save your work

Menu: File → Export as → PNG → click "Save". Also try File → Save as PSD to save the layered file — this keeps your layers editable, just like saving a Photoshop file!
