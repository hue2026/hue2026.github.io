🗝️ Vocabulary — 5 Key Terms

Know These Before We Watch

📈 Narrative Arc

The overall shape of a story — beginning, rising action, climax, and resolution. In Naruto, each arc (Chunin Exams, Pain's Assault) has its own complete arc inside the bigger story.

⏪ Flashback

A scene that jumps backward in time to reveal past events. In Demon Slayer, Tanjiro's memories of his father explain his breathing technique and give the present moment emotional weight.

👁️ Foreshadowing

Hints planted early that something important is coming later. In Attack on Titan, tiny details in Season 1 foreshadow massive plot twists seasons later — viewers only see them on rewatch.

⚡ Cliffhanger

Ending an episode at a peak moment of tension — forcing viewers to watch the next one. Attack on Titan is famous for ending episodes right when the most shocking thing happens.

🌱 Character Development

How a character changes — in beliefs, skills, or personality — over time due to challenges. Deku in My Hero Academia transforms from a quirkless, bullied kid into a confident hero through years of failure, training, and growth.

⏪ Technique: Flashback Demon Slayer · Mugen Train Arc

Why Flashbacks Work

A flashback is not just a "memory" — it's the writer telling us exactly what the audience needs to know at a precise emotional moment. In Demon Slayer, Tanjiro sees his father Tanjuro in a dream flashback while near death. This reveals that his father was also a demon slayer, explains why the king of demons feared the Kamado family, and transforms a fight scene into an emotionally devastating moment.

📌 Fact: Japanese anime uses flashbacks more than almost any other visual medium — averaging 3–5 per story arc in long-running shonen series.

👁️ Technique: Foreshadowing Attack on Titan

Attack on Titan – The Art of Foreshadowing (ViniiTube)

Chekhov's Gun in Anime

Foreshadowing plants clues the audience won't recognize until later. Author Hajime Isayama (AoT) said he wrote Season 1 already knowing the ending — meaning Eren's true ability as the Attack Titan is hinted in the very first episode, 87 episodes before it's revealed. Fans who rewatched the show found over 200 foreshadowing moments hidden throughout the series.

📌 Writing Tip: Foreshadowing works because the brain creates satisfaction when a hint pays off — it makes the audience feel smart for noticing it.

⚡ Technique: Cliffhanger Multiple Anime · Episode Endings

Why You Can't Stop Watching

A cliffhanger deliberately ends a scene or episode at the highest possible moment of tension — before the resolution. This exploits what psychologists call the Zeigarnik Effect: our brains remember unfinished tasks more strongly than completed ones. Anime uses this at episode endings, mid-episode breaks, and even between seasons, making viewers feel physically unable to stop watching.

📌 Fact: The Promised Neverland Episode 1 ends on one of the most shocking cliffhangers in anime history — viewers described it as "physically needing" to see what happened next.

🌱 Technique: Character Development My Hero Academia

How My Hero Academia Develops Its Characters (Storyteller's Notebook)

The Change We Root For

Character development is the engine of every great story. Izuku Midoriya starts as a boy with zero powers who cries watching heroes on TV. By Season 6, he is nearly destroying himself to protect others. What changed? Hundreds of moments of failure, pain, and choice — each one a storytelling decision. MHA shows development across ALL characters: Bakugo from bully to reluctant ally; Todoroki from cold loner to open teammate.

📌 Rule: Change must come from inside the character — forced by events, not given for free. A character who doesn't struggle doesn't develop.

📈 Technique: Narrative Arc Naruto · One Piece · MHA

The Shape of Every Story

Every arc follows the same 5-stage structure. In Naruto's Chunin Exams Arc (Episodes 20–67):

Introduction: Teams enter the exams — stakes are clear

Rising Action: Forest of Death, matches, Orochimaru appears

Climax: Naruto vs. Neji — "I'll change your destiny!"

Falling Action: Village invasion begins

Resolution: Team bonds formed; new arcs seeded

What Is An Arc In Anime? (The Animation Reel)

📌 Fact: One Piece has run continuously since 1997 and contains over 30 named arcs — yet each follows the same 5-stage structure.

✏️ Studio Time — 15 min Create Your Storyboard

Your Anime Scene

Storyboard an original anime scene (4–6 panels) that includes:

  • ✅ At least 2 storytelling techniques from today's vocabulary
  • ✅ A character from your course project (or create a new one)
  • ✅ Labels in the margin naming the technique used in each panel
  • ✅ A brief caption per panel explaining what's happening

💡 Tip: Real anime storyboards (絵コンテ, e-konte) are rough stick-figure sketches. Quality of art does NOT matter — clarity of storytelling does.

? Before you draw: what is the single emotion you want your viewer to feel at the end of your scene?

Socratic Prompts While You Work

"If I removed the flashback panel, what meaning would the scene lose?"

"Where is the moment of highest tension? Is it in the right panel?"

"How does the camera angle change between panels? What does that say?"

"Does your character look different at the end than at the start? Why?"

"What is the ONE thing the audience must understand from this scene?"

🎯 Remember: A storyboard is a plan — it exists so you can change ideas cheaply, before animating. Be bold, then revise.

🤝 Share Your Storyboard Socratic Gallery Walk

How to Give Great Feedback

👀 Step 1: Look

Spend 60 seconds silently reading your partner's storyboard. Notice the techniques, the emotion, the character choices.

💬 Step 2: Name

Point to a specific panel and name the technique you see: "In panel 3, I see a flashback because…"

❓ Step 3: Question

Ask one genuine question: "What would happen if this panel came first instead?" or "Who is your audience for this scene?"

Sentence Starters for Peer Discussion:

"The technique I saw most clearly was…" "I wonder what would happen if…" "This made me feel _____ because…" "I disagree with this choice because…"
? After hearing feedback: what is ONE thing you would change — and why?
