📖 Vocabulary

Key Terms for Today

Anime

Japanese animation originating in the early 1900s; now a global $25B+ industry

Manga

Japanese comic books; the primary source material for most anime adaptations

Mecha

Genre featuring giant robots; pioneered by Gundam (1979) and Evangelion (1995)

Shōjo

Genre aimed at young women; focuses on emotion, relationships, and growth

Shōnen

Genre aimed at young men; focuses on action, friendship, and self-improvement

Cel Animation

Hand-drawn frames on transparent acetate sheets; used 1963–mid-1990s

🕰️ Origins

1907–1940s: Where It All Began

🎞️
1907 — Katsudō Shashin
Earliest known Japanese animation: a 2-second boy drawing kanji on a board. Found in a Kyoto flea market in 2005.
✏️
1917 — First Commercial Anime
Seitaro Kitayama and Junichi Kouchi produce short films inspired by Western animation (Méliès, Bray). Japan begins its own visual storytelling tradition.
⚔️
1940s — WWII Propaganda Films
Government-funded animations like Momotarō's Divine Sea Warriors (1945) — Japan's first feature-length anime — used the medium as war propaganda.
🌏
Context Question
Why might a government want to use animation during wartime?

Tetsuwan Atom manga, 1963

⭐ 1963 — The Birth of Modern Anime

Osamu Tezuka & Astro Boy

Why Tezuka Changed Everything

  • Introduced limited animation: 8 frames/sec vs Disney's 24 — made TV production affordable
  • Pioneered large expressive eyes to convey emotion — inspired by Betty Boop & Disney
  • Launched Japan's first weekly TV anime: Tetsuwan Atom (Astro Boy) Jan 1, 1963
  • 4,000 episodes directed over his career; called the "God of Manga"

💻 Digital Revolution

1990s–2000s: Cel → Digital

🎬 Key Transition Titles

1988 Akira — 327 colors, $11M budget, cel pinnacle
1995 Evangelion — last major cel TV series
1998 Cowboy Bebop — 1st fully digital TV anime
2000s Full digital — unlimited palette + 3D compositing

See the evolution in detail: ▶ youtube.com — Evolution of Anime Art Styles

Cel vs. Digital: The Shift

🖌 Cel Era (pre-1995)

Hand-painted acetate sheets; warm organic color; Neon Genesis Evangelion last major cel series (1995)

🖥 Digital Era (1995+)

Computer coloring; unlimited palette; 3D compositing; Cowboy Bebop (1998) first fully digital TV series

🌸 Culture & Context

Why Does Japan Tell Stories This Way?

⚔️

Post-WWII Trauma

Tezuka survived the firebombing of Osaka. His work reflects hopes for peace technology (Astro Boy) and anti-war themes.

🌿

Shinto & Nature

Shinto teaches all things have spirits. Ghibli's forests, rivers, and spirits directly reflect this worldview.

📈

Economic Miracle → Bust

1980s bubble economy funded lavish productions (Akira). The 1990s crash brought existential themes — Evangelion (1995) reflects societal collapse anxiety.

🫂

Collectivist Culture

Japanese society values group harmony. Anime heroes almost always succeed through teamwork, not individual genius alone.
