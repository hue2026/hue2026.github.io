🎨 Digital Art – Lesson 3

Understanding and Utilizing Layers in Digital Art

Today you will think like digital illustrators: you will explore what layers are, why artists use them, and how you can control them to build a multi-layered artwork in Photopea.

⏱️ About 60 minutes Grade 7–8 Tool: Photopea
Do Now – 5 minutes

Spot the Invisible Layers

Look carefully at a finished digital artwork on the board – what might be hiding on separate layers?

When you look at this artwork, which parts do you suspect were drawn on different layers, and why might the artist have separated them?

Quiet Think Time – 2 min

  • List at least three separate elements you notice (for example: background sky, main character, text, shadows).
  • Next to each element, jot a reason it might be helpful to keep it on its own layer.

Turn & Talk – 3 min

  • Share your list with a partner and compare ideas.
  • Where do you agree? Where do you disagree about what should be on its own layer?
Learning Roadmap

What You Will Learn & Use

By the end of class, you will know how to build and control layers to create cleaner, more flexible digital art.

Learning Objectives

  • Explain what a layer is in digital art software using your own words.
  • Use multiple layers (background, main object, details) to build an illustration in Photopea.
  • Control opacity, blending modes, and layer masks to add effects without destroying your work.

Materials & Tools

  • 💻 Laptop (1 per student).
  • 🌐 Access to Photopea (online editor).
  • 📽️ Teacher projector or SmartBoard.
  • 📚 Optional: Canva Design School & ArtStation Learning for extension.
Language of Layers

Vocabulary for Today

Understanding these words will help you make smart choices inside Photopea instead of just clicking around.

Layers

A stack of transparent sheets that can each hold different parts of your artwork.

Socratic prompt: If your drawing were a sandwich, which parts would live on separate “slices of bread” and why?

Opacity

How see‑through a layer is (0% = invisible, 100% = solid).

Socratic prompt: How might lowering opacity change the mood or depth of a background?

Blending Modes

Math rules that tell one layer how to mix its colors with the layer underneath.

Socratic prompt: Why might an artist choose Overlay instead of just painting with a lighter color?

Layer Masks

Tools that hide or reveal parts of a layer without erasing the pixels.

Socratic prompt: Why is “hiding” better than “erasing” when you are experimenting?

Core Idea

Thinking in Transparent Sheets

Many artists imagine layers as clear plastic sheets that can move, hide, or reorder without ruining the drawing.

Guided Explanation

  • Each layer can hold one “job” (background color, character line art, shading, text).
  • You can turn layers on/off like light switches to test different ideas.
  • Reordering layers changes which parts appear in front or behind.

Socratic Discussion

If you had to delete one part of your artwork later, which element would you definitely want on its own layer, and what problem are you trying to avoid?

Invite 2–3 students to share. Ask: “What happened last time you drew everything on one layer?”

Hands-on Start – 10 minutes

Getting Into Photopea

You will set up a new canvas, then decide–before you draw–which parts deserve their own layers.

Step‑by‑Step Directions

  1. Open photopea.com in your browser.
  2. Click New Project → choose a preset size (for example 1280×720).
  3. Rename the default layer “Background Color”.
  4. Create two more layers: “Main Object” and “Details”.

Socratic Checks

Before you start drawing, which layer do you think should be locked or protected first, and how will that help future you?

  • Ask: “If you forget to switch layers and draw on the wrong one, what problems might you run into?”
  • Invite students to predict mistakes out loud before they make them.
Visual Effects – 10 minutes

Opacity & Blending Modes in Action

Small changes in opacity and blending can dramatically change how layers interact without redrawing anything.

Teacher Demo

  • Select the “Background Color” layer and try lowering opacity from 100% to around 60%.
  • Show how text or a character pops more when the background is softer.
  • Switch the “Details” layer through blending modes like Multiply, Overlay, Screen.

Socratic Prompts

When you change a layer’s blending mode instead of its color, what stays the same and what actually changes in the picture?

  • Ask: “How could opacity help you test a night‑time vs. daytime version of the same scene?”
  • Challenge: “Which blending mode would you try for soft shadows, and why?”
Protect Your Work – 10 minutes

Layer Masks: Hide, Don’t Destroy

Masks let you experiment fearlessly because you can always bring pixels back.

Mini Demo

  1. Select your “Main Object” layer.
  2. Add a Layer Mask (white thumbnail appears).
  3. Paint on the mask with a soft black brush to hide parts of the object.
  4. Switch to white to reveal hidden parts again.

Socratic Reflection

In what situation would you choose a layer mask instead of the eraser tool, and how does that choice change your confidence while experimenting?

  • Ask: “Have you ever erased something and then wished you could get it back?”
  • Connect: “How is a mask like using pencil instead of pen on a test?”
Guided Practice – 15 minutes

Design Your Own Layered Artwork

You will now combine all of today’s ideas to build a small, multi-layered illustration in Photopea.

Required Layers

  • Layer 1 – Background (simple gradient or scene color).
  • Layer 2 – Main Object (character, plant, building, etc.).
  • Layer 3 – Details (highlights, small decorations, sparkles).
  • At least one layer that uses a blending mode or reduced opacity.
  • At least one layer with a layer mask to shape or fade an area.

Socratic Coaching Questions

As you work, which layer are you choosing on purpose before you draw each stroke, and what are you trying to protect by choosing that layer?

  • Circulate and ask: “If you had 5 more minutes, which new layer would you add, and what job would it do?”
  • Ask: “Is there anything on this layer that would be safer on its own new layer?”
Wrap Up – 10 minutes

Flattening & Reflecting on Layers

You will think about when to keep layers separate and when it is finally safe to merge them.

Flattening Talk

  • Explain that flattening merges layers into one image for saving or sharing.
  • Ask: “When would it be dangerous to flatten too early in your process?”
  • Show how to duplicate a file so one version stays layered.

If you had to send this artwork to a client or teacher, would you send the flattened version, the layered version, or both, and why?

Exit Ticket

On a sticky note or in a quick Google Form, answer in 1–2 sentences:

“How did using layers change your approach to creating digital art today?”

  • Encourage students to reference at least one vocabulary word: layer, opacity, blending mode, layer mask, flatten.
  • Invite 2–3 volunteers to share their answers with the class.
Keep Creating

Beyond Class: Level Up Your Layers

Your next challenge is to teach yourself a new layering skill and bring the results back to the studio.

At‑Home Exploration

  • Choose a tutorial on Canva Design School or ArtStation Learning that focuses on layers, masks, or blending.
  • Create a new layered artwork at home using at least 5 layers.
  • Save both a layered file and a flattened image.

Socratic Challenge

Next class, how will you explain to a peer one layering trick you learned on your own, and what problem does that trick help to solve in real artworks?

  • Prepare to show which layers you used and why you organized them that way.
  • Be ready to answer: “What would have gone wrong if you had drawn this all on one layer?”
