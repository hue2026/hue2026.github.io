📖 Vocabulary

Key Terms for Today

Globalization

The process by which cultures, economies, and media spread worldwide

Cultural Exchange

When ideas, art, or customs cross between different communities

Pop Culture

Entertainment and trends widely consumed by the general public

Cross-media Influence

When one art form (anime) shapes another (film, fashion, games)

Iconography

Recognizable visual symbols tied to a particular culture or genre

Soft Power

A nation's cultural influence over others through media and art — not force

🎬 Film & TV

How Has Anime Shaped Western Animation?

🌊 Avatar: The Last Airbender

Explicitly anime-inspired; creators cited Dragon Ball Z, FLCL, and Miyazaki. Fluid bending animation, moral grey characters — it changed what US cartoons could be.

🕷 Spider-Man: Into the Spider-Verse

Deliberately used manga-style Ben-Day dots, speed lines, and 12fps "anime smears." Won the Oscar for Best Animated Film (2019).

🥊 Creed III (2023)

Director Michael B. Jordan cited Hajime no Ippo and Baki. Fight scenes used anime camera angles and visual metaphors — first mainstream Hollywood film to do this openly.

💊 The Matrix (1999)

Wachowskis storyboarded scenes using Ghost in the Shell. The "bullet time" effect was borrowed from anime. They screened GitS for WB executives to pitch the concept.

🏰 Studio Ghibli

Spirited Away — A Story the Whole World Claimed

🌐 Big Question

As Anime Goes Global — Does It Stay Japanese?

✅

YES — It Stays Japanese

  • Shinto values persist in Ghibli's spirits and nature
  • Japanese concepts like "nakama" (chosen family) remain untranslatable
  • Production studios, creators, and funding remain in Japan
  • Cultural specificity (food, school life, social codes) is preserved

🌍

NO — It's Becoming Global

  • Genshin Impact: Chinese studio using anime aesthetics globally
  • Scott Pilgrim (Canadian) animated in Japanese anime style
  • Netflix co-productions alter content for Western audiences
  • Western fans now define what "feels like anime"

Where do you stand?

🎸 Course Anime: K-On!

K-On! — How a School Band Went Global

🎸 The Guitar Effect

  • Gibson and Fender reported 30%+ sales spikes in Japan after K-On! (2009)
  • Effect spread globally — UK & US music shops tracked "K-On! inquiries"
  • Created the "moe music anime" subgenre — now over 40 shows

🌍 Global Fan Clubs

Active K-On! tribute bands in Brazil, Indonesia, Germany, USA. Members learned Japanese to understand lyrics. Language learning as cultural bridge.

🎵 The Soft Power Formula

K-On! turned: Music → Language Learning → Tourism → Cultural Ambassador. Japanese tourism to K-On! filming locations grew 400% after the film.
